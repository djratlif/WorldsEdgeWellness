Therapeutic Approaches & Methods

I use an integrative approach, drawing from various evidence-based therapeutic modalities to best meet your individual needs and preferences.

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

CBT helps identify and change negative thought patterns and behaviors that contribute to emotional distress. This approach is particularly effective for anxiety, depression, and trauma-related symptoms.

Best for: Anxiety, depression, phobias, OCD, PTSD

Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT)

DBT focuses on building skills in emotional regulation, distress tolerance, mindfulness, and interpersonal effectiveness. It's especially helpful for managing intense emotions and improving relationships.

Best for: Emotional dysregulation, self-harm, relationship difficulties

Trauma-Informed Care

A specialized approach that recognizes and responds to the impact of traumatic stress. This method emphasizes safety, trustworthiness, and collaboration in the healing process.

Best for: PTSD, complex trauma, childhood abuse, recent traumatic events

Mindfulness-Based Interventions

Incorporates mindfulness practices to help develop present-moment awareness, reduce stress, and improve emotional regulation. These techniques can be integrated into various therapeutic approaches.

Best for: Stress reduction, anxiety, depression, chronic pain

Solution-Focused Therapy

A goal-oriented approach that focuses on solutions rather than problems. This method helps identify your strengths and resources to create positive change quickly and effectively.

Best for: Goal achievement, brief therapy needs, motivation building

Family Systems Therapy

Views problems within the context of family relationships and dynamics. This approach helps improve communication patterns and relationships within the family system.

Best for: Family conflicts, relationship issues, systemic problems
