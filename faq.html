Getting Started

How do I know if therapy is right for me?

+

Therapy can be beneficial for anyone who wants to improve their mental health, work through challenges, or simply gain a better understanding of themselves. You might consider therapy if you're experiencing:

  • Persistent feelings of sadness, anxiety, or overwhelm
  • Difficulty coping with life changes or stressful situations
  • Relationship problems or communication difficulties
  • Feeling stuck or wanting personal growth
  • Trauma or difficult past experiences affecting your daily life

Even if you're not in crisis, therapy can be a valuable tool for personal development and maintaining good mental health.

What should I expect in my first session?

+

Your first session, often called an intake or assessment session, is longer than regular sessions (usually 90 minutes). During this time, we'll:

  • Discuss what brought you to therapy and your current concerns
  • Review your personal history, including family, relationships, and past experiences
  • Talk about your goals for therapy
  • Go over confidentiality, policies, and answer any questions you have
  • Begin to develop a treatment plan tailored to your needs

Remember, you only share what you're comfortable sharing. This session is about getting to know each other and determining how I can best support you.

Do you offer telehealth/online sessions?

+

Yes! I offer secure, HIPAA-compliant telehealth sessions for clients who prefer online therapy or cannot attend in-person sessions. Telehealth can be just as effective as in-person therapy for many concerns.

To participate in telehealth sessions, you'll need:

  • A reliable internet connection
  • A computer, tablet, or smartphone with camera and microphone
  • A private space where you can speak freely
  • To be physically located in North Carolina during the session

The Therapy Process

How long does therapy take?

+

The length of therapy varies greatly depending on your individual needs, goals, and circumstances. Some people benefit from short-term therapy (8-12 sessions) focused on specific issues, while others prefer longer-term therapy for deeper exploration and growth.

Factors that influence therapy duration include:

  • The complexity of your concerns
  • Your personal goals and what you want to achieve
  • How you respond to treatment
  • Your commitment to the process
  • External factors and life circumstances

We'll regularly check in about your progress and adjust our approach as needed. You're always in control of when to end therapy.

How often will we meet?

+

Most clients start with weekly sessions, which allows for consistent progress and momentum. As you begin to feel better and develop coping skills, we might move to bi-weekly or monthly sessions.

Session frequency depends on:

  • The severity of your symptoms
  • Your current level of functioning
  • Your personal preferences and schedule
  • Your treatment goals
  • Insurance coverage or financial considerations

We'll work together to find a schedule that works best for your needs and circumstances.

What if I don't feel comfortable or connected with you?

+

The therapeutic relationship is crucial to successful therapy, and it's completely normal if you don't feel a connection right away. I encourage open communication about this.

If you're not feeling comfortable:

  • Please share your concerns with me directly - this can actually strengthen our therapeutic relationship
  • We can discuss what might help you feel more comfortable
  • Sometimes it takes a few sessions to build trust and rapport
  • If after giving it some time you still don't feel it's a good fit, I'm happy to provide referrals to other therapists

Your comfort and progress are my top priorities, and there's no shame in finding a different therapist if that's what's best for you.

Will you give me advice or tell me what to do?

+

My role isn't to give you advice or tell you what to do. Instead, I'll help you explore your thoughts, feelings, and options so you can make decisions that are right for you.

In therapy, I will:

  • Ask thoughtful questions to help you gain insight
  • Provide tools and coping strategies
  • Help you identify patterns and connections
  • Support you in exploring different perspectives
  • Teach skills for managing emotions and relationships

You are the expert on your own life, and my job is to support you in discovering your own answers and building confidence in your decision-making abilities.

Practical Information

Do you accept insurance?

+

Yes, I accept several major insurance plans including:

  • Blue Cross Blue Shield
  • Aetna
  • Cigna
  • UnitedHealthcare
  • Medicare

I recommend calling your insurance company to verify your mental health benefits, including:

  • Your copay amount
  • Whether you have a deductible that applies to mental health services
  • How many sessions are covered per year
  • Whether you need a referral from your primary care doctor

If you don't have insurance or prefer to pay out-of-pocket, I offer sliding scale fees based on financial need.

What is your cancellation policy?

+

I understand that life happens and sometimes you need to reschedule. My cancellation policy is:

  • 24-hour notice: Please provide at least 24 hours notice to cancel or reschedule without charge
  • Late cancellations: Cancellations with less than 24 hours notice will be charged the full session fee
  • No-shows: If you don't show up and haven't contacted me, you'll be charged the full session fee
  • Emergencies: True emergencies are handled on a case-by-case basis

Insurance typically doesn't cover missed appointment fees, so these would be your responsibility. I'm always willing to work with you if you're having difficulties with scheduling.

Is everything I say confidential?

+

Yes, confidentiality is a cornerstone of therapy. Everything you share with me is protected by law and professional ethics. I cannot share information about you without your written consent.

There are a few legal exceptions where I may be required to break confidentiality:

  • If you pose an imminent danger to yourself or others
  • If there's suspected abuse of a child, elderly person, or disabled person
  • If ordered by a court of law
  • If you sign a release allowing me to share specific information

I will always discuss these situations with you first whenever possible and only share the minimum information necessary to ensure safety.

Can I contact you between sessions?

+

Yes, you can contact me between sessions for brief check-ins, scheduling, or urgent concerns. Here's how:

  • Email: Best for non-urgent matters and scheduling
  • Phone: For more urgent concerns (I'll return calls within 24 hours)
  • Text: Only for scheduling or brief logistical matters

Please note:

  • I'm not available 24/7 for crisis situations
  • If you're having a mental health emergency, call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room
  • For crisis support, you can call the National Suicide Prevention Lifeline at 988
  • Extended phone conversations may be billed as a session

Common Concerns

I'm worried about the stigma of going to therapy. What should I know?

+

It's completely understandable to have concerns about stigma. The good news is that attitudes toward mental health and therapy have changed dramatically in recent years.

Consider these facts:

  • About 1 in 5 adults in the US receives mental health treatment each year
  • Many successful, high-functioning people regularly attend therapy
  • Therapy is increasingly seen as a form of self-care and personal development
  • Your therapy attendance is completely confidential

Remember, seeking help is a sign of strength, not weakness. It shows self-awareness and a commitment to your well-being. You don't have to tell anyone you're in therapy if you don't want to.

What if I cry or get emotional during sessions?

+

Crying and showing emotion in therapy is completely normal and actually healthy! My office is a safe space where all emotions are welcome.

Here's what you should know:

  • Emotions are information - they tell us what's important to us
  • Expressing emotions can be healing and provide relief
  • I'm trained to support you through difficult emotions
  • There's no "right" way to do therapy - some people cry, others don't
  • Tissues are always available, and there's no judgment

If you're worried about getting emotional, we can talk about coping strategies and ways to feel more comfortable expressing your feelings.

I'm not sure I'm "sick enough" for therapy. Do I really need it?

+

This is one of the most common concerns I hear, and I want to assure you that you don't need to be in crisis to benefit from therapy.

Therapy can help with:

  • Everyday stress and life challenges
  • Personal growth and self-improvement
  • Relationship enhancement
  • Career and life transitions
  • Building better coping skills
  • Increasing self-awareness and emotional intelligence

Think of therapy like going to the gym for your mental health - you don't have to be physically sick to benefit from exercise, and you don't have to be in crisis to benefit from therapy. Prevention and maintenance are just as important as treatment.

What if therapy doesn't work for me?

+

It's natural to wonder about this, especially if you've had negative experiences in the past or are feeling skeptical about the process.

Here's what research tells us:

  • Therapy is effective for most people who engage in the process
  • The therapeutic relationship is one of the strongest predictors of success
  • Different approaches work better for different people and problems
  • Your active participation greatly influences outcomes

If you're not seeing progress:

  • We'll regularly check in about how you're feeling about our work together
  • We can adjust our approach or try different techniques
  • I can provide referrals to other therapists who might be a better fit
  • Sometimes it takes time to see changes - healing isn't always linear
